# Insights Summary

## Executive / Business Overview

- **Total Orders:** ~20,000 orders processed across all factories
- **Total Revenue:** $19B across the full order portfolio
- **Current On-Time Delivery (OTD) Rate:** 64.46% — well below the 80% target
- **Late Orders:** ~7,090 orders delivered after promised date
- **Delivered Quantity:** 47.7M KG total; **Late Delivered Quantity:** 15.8M KG (≈33% of volume)
- **Volume Conformance:** 49.35% — fewer than half of all orders are delivered within the agreed quantity tolerance
- **Average Delivery Delay:** ~5 days across all late orders
- **Current Late Delivery Cost:** €11.8M annually (15.8M KG × €0.75/KG penalty)
- **Revenue at Risk:** $5.3B tied to late or non-conforming orders

---

## Customer Insights

- **Total Customers:** 307 unique customers
- **Customers Affected by Delays:** 218 out of 307 (≈71% of the customer base experiences at least one late delivery)
- **Customer Risk Exposure:** Several customers face 100% delay rates on their orders, indicating critical service failure
- **Revenue Concentration Risk:** The top customer — **Schweizer** ($6.9B total spend) — is among those impacted by delays, representing extreme churn risk; other high-value accounts include Wiegand ($830M), Jurgens ($790M), Brand AG ($610M), and Götz ($560M)
- **Average Revenue per Customer:** approximately $62M (total revenue $19B ÷ 307 customers); average order value is ~$950K per order ($19B ÷ 20,000 orders)
- **Volume Conformance:** 49.35% of orders fall within the agreed quantity tolerance — meaning more than half of customers receive incorrect delivery volumes
- **Revenue at Risk from Late Orders:** $5.3B — customer dissatisfaction threatens long-term relationships and recurring revenue

**Key Risk:** High-value customers experiencing repeated delays face high churn probability. Protecting Schweizer and the next tier of accounts (Wiegand, Jurgens, Brand AG) should be a priority.

## Factory Insights

- **Total Factories:** 6 (Aachen, Bonn, Crefeld, Essen, and others)
- **Factory performance varies significantly** — Aachen leads while others lag:

| Factory | Orders | OTD %  | Volume Conformance % | Avg Delay (Days) |
|---------|--------|--------|----------------------|-----------------|
| Aachen  | 8,138  | 93.65% | 60.41%               | 7.80            |
| Crefeld | 1,415  | 86.93% | 72.16%               | 8.24            |
| Bonn    | 1,166  | 86.36% | 38.77%               | 10.78           |
| Essen   | —      | lower  | —                    | higher          |

- **Aachen** is the top-performing factory with 93.65% OTD and should serve as the **benchmark for best-practice replication**
- **Bonn** has the lowest volume conformance (38.77%) and longest average delay (10.78 days) — a key improvement target
- **Essen** generates the highest revenue among all factories but carries higher delay rates, creating a high-impact improvement opportunity
- **Warehouse automation:** 99.75% of warehouse capacity is **non-automated**; only 0.25% is automated — this is a root cause of systemic delays

**Key Finding:** Aachen's practices (93.65% OTD) can be replicated across factories to raise the overall performance floor.

## Warehouse Insights

- **Automated Warehouses:** ~79.6% OTD — significantly above the company average of 64.46%
- **Non-Automated Warehouses:** ~49.4% OTD — well below the company average and the 80% target
- **Performance Gap:** Automated warehouses outperform non-automated warehouses by ~30 percentage points (79.6% vs. 49.4%)
- Nearly all warehouse capacity (99.75%) is non-automated, creating a company-wide structural disadvantage
- Expanding warehouse automation is the single highest-leverage operational improvement available

**Key Finding:** Warehouse automation is the most impactful lever for improving OTD. A targeted investment in automated warehouse capacity could close the gap to the 80% OTD target.

## Overall Insight

WoodCorp's delivery performance problem is **systematic and multi-dimensional**, stemming from three compounding root causes:

1. **Warehouse infrastructure:** 99.75% non-automated warehouses contribute to systemic delays; automated warehouses achieve ~79.6% OTD vs. ~49.4% for non-automated — a 30 percentage-point gap
2. **Factory inconsistency:** Aachen demonstrates 93.65% OTD is achievable; replicating its practices to Bonn and Essen can uplift network-level performance
3. **Logistics partner mix:** DB Schenker (0% OTD on crates) and FedEx (42–53% OTD) are anchoring the overall rate downward; reallocating volume to DHL would provide immediate gains

Addressing these three levers in parallel — warehouse automation investment, factory benchmarking, and delivery partner rebalancing — can realistically close the gap from 64.46% to the 80% OTD target, **saving approximately €6M annually** and protecting $5.3B in at-risk revenue from high-value customers.
