# Insights Summary – WoodCorp O2C Analysis

> Data source: 19,953 orders · 210,135 activity events · 309 unique customers

---

## 1. Delivery Performance

- Overall OTD is **64.46 %** – well below the 80 % industry target.
- **7,091 orders (35.54 %)** were delivered late, carrying an average delay of
  **4.67 days** on late shipments.
- Volume conformance stands at only **49.35 %**, meaning half of all orders are
  delivered outside the contractually agreed quantity tolerance.

## 2. Financial Impact

- Current late-delivery penalty cost: **€11,824,769 per year** (€0.75 / kg × 15.77 M kg late).
- Revenue at risk from late orders: **€68,619,901** (32.7 % of total revenue).
- Reaching OTD 80 % would save approximately **€5.17 M per year** in penalties.

## 3. Customer Insights

- **219 out of 309 customers (70.9 %)** have experienced at least one late delivery.
- Top revenue customers show alarming late rates: Götz (48.1 %), Becker (46.8 %),
  Meißner GmbH (55.4 %).
- High-value accounts Brand AG (€19.5 M) and Götz (€17 M) represent significant
  churn risk given their elevated delay exposure.

## 4. Factory Insights

- **Essen** (€111.8 M, 65.6 % OTD) and **Aachen** (€85.4 M, 61.4 % OTD) account
  for 93 % of revenue and are the two worst-performing major factories.
- Aachen has the highest positive average delay (+0.8 days), signalling a structural
  production or loading bottleneck.
- **Crefeld** (70.1 % OTD) and **Duisburg** (69.2 % OTD, 85 % volume conformance)
  are the best-performing factories among significant sites.

## 5. Warehouse Insights

- Automated warehouses achieve **79.6 % OTD** – near the 80 % target.
- Non-automated warehouses (99.8 % of all orders) achieve only **64.4 % OTD**.
- Warehouse automation is a high-impact long-term investment to close the OTD gap.

## 6. Logistics / Carrier Insights

- **DHL** is the highest-performing carrier at **75.5 % OTD**.
- **FedEx** (48.3 % OTD) and **DB Schenker** (6.2 % OTD) are underperforming
  and should be reviewed for contract renegotiation or replacement.
- UPS, handling 90 % of orders at 64.4 % OTD, is the primary improvement lever
  because even a small improvement has an outsized effect on total penalties.

## 7. Process-Level Insights

- **"Change production start date"** is recorded 23,693 times (>1 per order),
  indicating pervasive production scheduling instability.
- **"Change delivery date"** occurs in 68.5 % of orders, masking the true OTD
  performance and creating customer uncertainty.
- **"Credit order block"** interrupts 10.1 % of orders, potentially cascading into
  delivery delays.
- **"Change quantity"** in 97.9 % of orders explains the 49.35 % volume conformance rate.

## 8. Overall Conclusion

WoodCorp's delivery issues are **systematic and multi-dimensional**: they stem from
production scheduling instability, non-automated warehouse infrastructure, underperforming
logistics partners, and chronic order modifications.  A targeted programme addressing
Aachen factory scheduling, FedEx/DB Schenker carrier relationships, and warehouse
automation can realistically move OTD from 64.5 % to 80 %+, unlocking **€5.2 M in
annual savings** and protecting **€68.6 M of revenue at risk**.
